The History Of The Game

The game of hide and seek has been played across different countries for generations, with its origins shrouded in mystery. However, a similar game called apodidraskinda was recorded in Greece in the 2nd century by Julius Pollux. Unlike the modern version of hide and seek, in apodidraskinda one player would hide while all other team members sought them out. This game was widely popular in numerous countries, and its variations were adapted to suit different cultures.

Rules Of Hide And Seek Game:

  1. You need to have at least 2 players to play this game
  2. The team need to pick one team player who will be seeking other team players
  3. Other team members will be hiding
  4. Once the roles have been decided, the seeker need to close his/her eyes and count to 100
  5. When the seeker start counting, other team members need to hide.

Mission of the game:

  • The hider's mission is to find a hiding place where the seeker will not be able to find them quickly. The purpose of the game is to avoid being found and to be the last person to be discovered
  • The last found, is the winner
  • The first found, lost the game and becomes seeker in the next round

Recommended Tips And Strategies

For hiders:

If you are a hider, try to think in advance about the places, you could potentially hide. In my experience, I would usually hide in the places where it is hard for others to believe that somebody can fit in that place. For example, inside sofa or small box or some place that has a lot of clothes where it is hard to notice you.
